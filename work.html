Work Experience

Carolyn has been a Healthcare Operations Analyst at the Center for Healthcare Engineering and Patient Safety (CHEPS) since September 2019. Through the program, she's been able to work on projects such as improving eye care access for veterans, scheduling residents efficently during the time of COVID, and improving the appointment making process for patients with Chronic Liver disease. CHEPS is also partnered with the American University Hospital in Beirut, Lebanon and working on a resident scheduling project through international collaboration.

She is currently the project lead of BlueLab Nicaragua, a student engineering team dedicated to enabling accessibility through sustainable engineering. The team is currently working on creating an assistive seating device to improve comfort and support the posture of children with cerebral palsy in Nicaragua. One important priority is that the device is sustainably made through locally sourced materials, easily fixed, and durable. The team is currently partnered with the non-profit, FNE International.

She was also the a digital and field organizer for Andrew Yang's 2020 Presidential campaign - working with constituents from all backgrounds, political parties, and socioeconomic statuses. The Ann Arbor chapter worked to canvass, phonebank, host events, and spread awareness about the importance of basic income and a human centered economy.
